## What we're building

### 1. Auth migration (Firebase → Clerk)

See VEC-52. This is the critical path item — everything else depends on auth being
stable. Clerk supports SAML/OIDC natively. Migration is incremental: new signups
on Clerk first, existing customers migrated in batches. Firebase stays live in
read-only fallback until all customers are confirmed on Clerk.

Do not let anyone ship the Firebase removal before all 6 existing customers
are migrated. This is a hard rule.

### 2. Per-org retention configuration

See VEC-47. Today retention is global (90 days raw, 2 years aggregated). Meridian
needs 1 year raw. We're building per-org config so this is extensible — future
Enterprise customers will want the same thing.

> **Note from Dana (Jan 28):** Marcus and I disagree on implementation approach
> here. I want the retention config to be customer-facing (visible in the settings UI).
> Marcus wants it internal-only for now ("we don't want customers managing their own
> TTL policies"). I think we're leaving value on the table but I've agreed to ship
> internal-only for v1 and revisit. Flagging so the decision is documented.

### 3. Security questionnaire

See VEC-58. Nina is coordinating. 147 questions, due Jan 31. The questionnaire
is the gate before the SOW gets signed — Rachel won't move forward without it.

### 4. SOW review and execution

See VEC-61. Orion Legal has the draft. Key commercial terms:

- $2,800/mo, 12-month term
- 99.9% uptime SLA (vs our standard 99.5% for Cascade)
- Financial credits: 5% of monthly fee per 0.1% below SLA, capped at 30%
- Termination: 60-day written notice

## What we're NOT building (for Meridian)

  engineering metrics, not PHI. If this changes, it's a separate conversation.
- EU data residency — not applicable, Meridian is US-only
- Full APM / distributed tracing — out of scope for Vectrix entirely

## Risks

**1. Clerk migration slips**
If VEC-52 slips past Feb 28, the SAML SSO demo we've promised Alan can't happen.
This is the #1 risk. Marcus is watching it daily.

**2. Rachel kills the deal on SOC 2**
We've managed this with the commitment letter approach. If Rachel changes her
position and requires Type II before signing, we have no good answer. Priya is
managing the relationship.

**3. SLA exposure**
99.9% uptime is a higher bar than our current 99.7% track record (Q4 2025).
The Feb 20 incident (45 min of stale data for Cascade) would have triggered
credits under the Meridian SLA. Jordan is working on ClickHouse maintenance
window coordination to close this gap.
